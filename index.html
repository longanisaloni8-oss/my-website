Unakoti Rock Carvings

A hidden treasure of ancient Indian art carved beautifully into nature itself

About Unakoti

Unakoti is one of the most unique historical sites in India, known for its massive rock carvings of Hindu gods and goddesses. These carvings are directly created on natural rocks and hills, which makes the place very different from other monuments. The site is surrounded by forests and greenery, giving it a peaceful and natural environment.

The artistic work seen here shows the creativity and skills of ancient craftsmen. Even today, the carvings attract visitors because of their size and detailed design.

Location & History

Unakoti is located in the state of Tripura in northeast India. The carvings are believed to be from the 7th to 9th centuries, making them very old and historically important.

The name “Unakoti” means “one less than a crore”. According to legend, Lord Shiva turned other gods into stone when they failed to wake up on time, leaving one less than a crore.

Historical Importance

Unakoti represents ancient Indian culture, art, and religion. The carvings include different forms of Lord Shiva and other deities. The most famous carving is the giant face of Lord Shiva, which is very large and detailed.

It shows how ancient people used nature to create art instead of building separate structures, making it a unique heritage site.

Interesting Facts

  • The name Unakoti means “one less than a crore”.
  • The carvings are made directly on rocks.
  • The Shiva face is about 30 feet high.
  • It is a less explored tourist place.
  • It is surrounded by forests and hills.
